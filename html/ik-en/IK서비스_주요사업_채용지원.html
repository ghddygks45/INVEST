The government and KOTRA undertake various online and offline projects in order to raise awareness of excellent foreign-invested companies and create opportunities to connect young Korean talent with foreign-invested companies. Two important projects aimed at employment support are the Job Expo and the Job Fair for foreign-invested companies.

Job Expo for Foreign-Invested Companies

The Job Expo is the largest and oldest event with the longest history that has supported employment of foreign-invested companies since 2006. Over 150 foreign-invested companies participate in the expo. The expo consists of recruitment halls (1:1 in-depth interviews, job consulting) and additional events (job information sessions, employment consulting, special lectures, etc.).

Job Fair for Foreign-Invested Companies

The Job Fair takes place during the biggest event week for foreign investment that is hosted around November every year. In-depth interviews are conducted for employment, and over 90 foreign-invested companies participate in the fair each year. To allow for hiring talent year round, online job fairs exclusive for foreign-invested companies are open on recruitment websites.

Other Support

The Korea Industrial Technology Association operates a recruitment center for scientists and engineers in order to meet the needs of foreign-invested companies that seek to hire excellent talent with educational backgrounds in natural sciences and engineering. The Ministry of Employment and Labor and the Korea Employment Information Service operate Worknet, a recruitment website that collects job information from private job portals and local government job portals. The Korea Industrial Complex Corporation hosts online and offline job fairs and operates KICOXJOB in order to recruit talent for local industrial complexes.

The establishment of foreign-invested companies pursuant to the Foreign Investment Promotion Act consists of the following steps: notification of foreign investment, remittance of investment funds, registration of corporation, acquisition of authorization and permission, notification of corporation, registration of business license, and registration of the foreign-invested company. The procedure applied to foreign-invested companies is the same as that for domestic corporations, with the exception of two additional steps—notification of foreign investment and registration of the foreign-invested company. The whole procedure takes two weeks, and a request for visa issuance can be made once registration has been completed.
※ Related laws: Articles 5 and 21 of the Foreign Investment Promotion Act, Articles 6 and 27 of the Enforcement Decree of the Act, Articles 2 and 17 of the Enforcement Rules of the Act

Send notification of foreign investment (KOTRA, foreign exchange bank)

  • takes : immediately
  • A foreign investor is first required to send pre-notification of the foreign investment to either KOTRA (via the Foreign Investor Support Center in KOTRA headquarters or an overseas investment hub office) or a foreign exchange bank.

Register corporate capital increase (court registry office)

  • takes : within two to three days
  • A foreign investor may remit investment funds by telegraphic transfer to a temporary bank account at a domestic foreign exchange bank or hand-carry the foreign currency through customs. In the case of the latter, the investor must declare the funds at the customs office and receive a declaration of currency or monetary instruments. In principle, remitted investment funds must undergo the payment of stocks procedure, and a certificate of deposit of payment of stocks must be submitted to the court. If the value of a company is less than KRW 1 billion, the investor may instead open a bank account under the name of the investor and submit a certificate of balance to the court. In this case, funds can be freely withdrawn for business purposes from the following day.

Send notification of corporation (court registry office)

  • takes : within 2~3 days
  • The court registry office receives the application for corporation registration and processes it within two to three days.
  • forms and required documents from the Online Supreme Court Registry Office.

Register corporate capital increase (court registry office)

  • takes : differs depending on permit
  • When deemed necessary for business, a foreign investor is required to acquire the authorization and permission from a competent authority such as a district office, a public health center, or the Ministry of Food and Drug Safety.
  • The processing period may vary depending on the types and forms of authorization and permission.

Notification of Corporation and Registration of Business License(tax office)

  • takes : within 4~5 days
  • A foreign investor may send a notification of corporation and register a business license to any tax office in Korea, regardless of the jurisdiction. The processing period is three days.

Open a corporate bank account (foreign exchange bank)

  • takes : immediately
  • A foreign investor is required to open a corporate bank account at a foreign exchange bank. The bank account is opened immediately, but it is recommended that investors carefully choose the bank as opening an additional account at another bank will be restricted for twenty business days.

Register the foreign-invested company (agency where the first notification form was filed)

  • takes : immediately
  • As the last step for a foreign investment, a foreign-invested company must be registered to the agency where the first notification was filed (KOTRA or a foreign exchange bank).
  • The registration must be completed within 60 days after the payment of the object of investment.

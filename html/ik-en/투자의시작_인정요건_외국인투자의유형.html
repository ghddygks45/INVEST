Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) is defined as an investment with an investment threshold contributed by foreigners amounting to at least KRW 100 million and being 10% or more of the total amount. Foreigners are able to do business in Korea without restraint, except as otherwise prescribed by regulations.
Types of FDI include the acquisition of stocks of domestic companies, long-term loans (five or more years) from parent companies outside Korea, and investments in non-profit corporations in the fields of science and technology.
Cases in which a foreign company uses unappropriated earned surpluses for building new or additional factories are now also considered FDI (to be enforced by 2020). Objects of investment to acquire stocks or shares include foreign currency, capital goods, proceeds from acquired stocks, etc., and industrial property rights.

Acquisition of Stocks or Shares of Domestic Companies

  • Acquisition of stocks or shares newly issued by a Korean corporation or a company run by a Korean national
  • Acquisition of stocks or shares previously issued by a Korean corporation or a company run by a Korean national

Long-term Loans

A long-term loan with maturity of five or more years, based on the loan maturity prescribed in the first loan contract, by an overseas parent company of a foreign-invested company, a foreign company or investor that made capital contribution, or a company with a capital investment relationship with such a company or individual.

Investment of Unappropriated Earned Surpluses

Reinvestment by a foreign-invested company of an unappropriated earned surplus into certain uses, such as building new or additional facilities. (In this case, a foreign-invested company is regarded as a foreigner, and the foreign investment amount is calculated by multiplying the investment ratio.) (to be enforced by 2020.)

Contribution to Non-Profit Organizations, etc.

A contribution to a non-profit organization or company is recognized as FDI when the amount of foreign contribution is KRW 50 million or more, accounts for 10% or more of the total contribution amount, and meets all of the following conditions:
The contribution is made to a non-profit organization or company in the field of science and technology which has an independent research facility, and which meets any one of the following conditions
  • There are five or more full-time research staff members who hold master’s degrees or higher in the field of science and technology or hold bachelor’s degrees in the field of science and technology with research careers of three or more years.
  • The non-profit organization’s business should be classified as Research and Experimental Development on Natural Sciences and Engineering under the Korean Standard Industrial Classification.
An investment is made to one of the non-profit organizations listed below and is recognized as foreign investment by the Foreign Investment Committee
  • The non-profit organization is established for the purpose of promoting science, art, medical service, or education. It continues its businesses in order to develop professionals and expand global exchanges in the relevant fields.
  • The non-profit organization is a regional office of an international organization that is engaged in international cooperation projects between civilians or governments.
※ Related laws: Article 2.1.2 of the Foreign Investment Promotion Act, Article 2.2, 2.4~7 of the Enforcement Decree of the Foreign Investment Promotion Act

Industrial Complexes

Non-manufacturers have more freedom with location selection. In order for a non-manufacturing company to move into an industrial complex, it must fall into the category of non-manufacturers prescribed by the basic management plan of the targeted industrial complex. The non-manufacturing industries that are eligible to occupy industrial complexes as prescribed by the Enforcement Decree of the Industrial Direct Activation and Factory Establishment Act are listed below. Applicants must check the basic management plan of the targeted industrial complex for confirmation. There are no restrictions on non-manufacturers unless they choose to occupy an industrial complex.

Individual Locations

Logistics Complexes
A logistics complex is a complex of land and facilities designated and developed in order to collectively establish and promote logistics and supportive facilities, which can be used to transport, collect, load and unload, sort, process, assemble, clear customs, store, and sell cargo as well as process related information. In seaport-type free trade zones, companies engaged in the loading and unloading, transport, storage, display, compound logistics (packing, repairing, processing, fabricating), and international logistics may take occupancy.
· Seaport-type free trade zones: Port of Busan, Port of Gwangyang, Port of Incheon, Port of Pyeongtaek-Dangjin, Port of Pohang
※ Related law: The Act on Designation and Management of Free Trade Zones
Knowledge Industry Centers
A knowledge industry center is a multi-story complex which can be concurrently occupied by manufacturing, knowledge-based, and information and communications businesses as well as supportive facilities. It is a collective building with at least three floors above ground and able to accommodate six or more businesses.
The following facilities may occupy knowledge industry centers:
  • facilities for manufacturing, knowledge-based businesses, information and communications businesses, and other businesses as prescribed separately;
  • facilities for venture businesses prescribed by the Act on Special Measures for the Promotion of Venture Businesses; and
  • facilities for supporting production of occupants as prescribed separately.
※ Related laws: Article 28.5 of the Industrial Cluster Development and Factory Establishment Act, Article 36.4 of the Enforcement Decree of the Act
Business Incubation Centers
A business incubation center is a special incubator that helps to raise the success rate of start-ups by offering space, management skills, and information to prospective start-ups or early start-ups possessing ideas and skills. It serves as a forward base for promoting start-ups, small- and medium-sized businesses, and venture businesses.
Companies that occupy business incubation centers can secure rental workplaces and use common facilities at an affordable cost, consequently lessening their financial burden. Various services, such as management, technology, and legal consulting, are also available.
In the case of incubators at universities and public research institutes, the Ministry of Small and Medium Venture Businesses supports up to 80% of construction costs (up to KRW 1 billion per operator).
Creative Economy Innovation Centers
Nineteen regions across the country operate creative economy innovation centers which support local talent in commercializing their creative ideas and starting businesses. The centers assist start-up mentoring, education, competition, and seminars; investor relations (IR); networking among local start-ups and businesses; marketing and sales channel promotion; and global expansion.

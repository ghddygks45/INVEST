Seamless and Affordable Electric Supply

According to the World Bank’s Doing Business 2019 report, Korea ranked 2nd in the category of Getting Electricity. The result highlights that electricity is supplied through a fast and simple process at an affordable price.
International Energy Agency (IEA) said Korea’s average cost of utilities per person (tax included: 8.02pence/ KWh) is the lowest of 28 OECD members as of 2019. Energy prices in Korea remains merely half the OECD average and 30% of that in Germany (tax included: 26.17 pence/ KWh) which tops in the global energy price index.
※ Source: IEA “Domestic electricity prices in the IEA, 2020”

Active Application for Intellectual Property Rights

The number of applications for intellectual property rights in Korea is the 4th largest in the world, following China, the US, and Japan, and Korea ranks 5th in the world in Patent Cooperation Treaty (PCT) patent applications. In addition, Resident patent applications filed per GDP, far outdistancing other leading countries, showing Korea’s excellence in application for intellectual property rights.

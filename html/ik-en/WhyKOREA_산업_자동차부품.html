  • Competitive Automotive Parts Industry The World’s 5th Largest Automobile Production Base, Producing 4 Million Vehicles Annually Close
    Competitive Automotive Parts Industry The World’s 5th Largest Automobile Production Base, Producing 4 Million Vehicles Annually
    Korea is the 5th largest automobile producer in the world, capable of producing over 4 million automobiles annually. Half of the production in Korea is sold to the domestic market, and the other half is exported worldwide. In 2021, due to the shortage of automotive semiconductors, a total of 3.46 million units were produced, of which 2.05 million units (60%), were exported.
    Korea is rapidly recovering from the COVID-19 crisis, thanks to its stable social structure and infrastructure. It is supported by a strong domestic market of 1.8 million units a year, which is the 10th largest in the world.
    Korean parts manufacturers are growing into global companies in line with their production volume, and many well-known overseas companies are operating base factories in Korea. The list of the world's top 100 auto parts makers in 2021 (based on sales) showed that nine Korean parts makers were listed. The sales of these nine companies amounted to USD 55.2 billion, accounting for 7.4% of the top 100 parts makers' sales. One more company was added to the list, up from eight companies in 2020. Yura, a supplier of electronic modules and wiring systems, grew significantly during the period of the transition to electric vehicles, and reached 78th place in 2021.

    In line with the global trend, the Korean automobile industry is also rapidly transforming toward electrification.
    The total ratio of eco-friendly vehicles represented by hybrids (HEV), plug-in hybrids (PHEV), electric vehicles (EV), and hydrogen vehicles (FCEV) rose from 9.4% in 2019 to 12.7% in 2020 and 18.6% in 2021, in terms of production.
    The number of units sold in Korea recorded 7.9% of all vehicles in 2019, 11.9% in 2020, and exceeded 20% in 2021. Pure eco-friendly vehicles, such as EVs and FCEVs, exceeded 2% in 2019 and 6% in 2021 in the domestic market. The prevailing prediction is that the transition to eco-friendly vehicles will accelerate in the future.
    Hyundai Motor Group is the only company in the world that mass-produces hydrogen vehicles for both passenger and commercial use. That is why many foreign companies with hydrogen technologies are interested in Korea's hydrogen industry.

  • Continuous Investment by Global Auto Parts Companies Open
    Continuous Investment by Global Auto Parts Companies
    Most of the world's top 100 auto parts makers have entered Korea, mainly supplying core high-tech parts. In the past two years, continuous investment has been made in the production of parts for future vehicles, such as EV motors/batteries, hydrogen fuel cells/tanks, and autonomous driving sensors.
    The Korean government is giving full support to foster the eco-friendly future car industry, and Korea is emerging as a production base for future cars.
  • Strengthening of Government Policies To Lead the Global Future Car Market Open
    Strengthening of Government Policies To Lead the Global Future Car Market
    The Korean government has developed various strategies to make a transition to future vehicles, such as electric vehicles, hydrogen vehicles, and autonomous vehicles. Measures include expanding subsidies for EV purchases, support for building electric/hydrogen charging facilities, and support for parts companies targeting future vehicles. In February 2021, Korea implemented the world's first hydrogen vehicle law, taking the lead in the development of the hydrogen vehicle industry.
    Government technology/R&D support for electric power vehicle-related parts
    (EV) Driving distance improvement (400km → 600km), charging time reduction (200kW → 400kW), and advancement of core component technology (USD 450 million worth of preliminary feasibility study in progress))
    Core parts : Battery cells, battery modules, battery management systems (BMS: Battery Management System), vehicle-mounted charging modules (OBC: On Board Charger)
    IT-based plans to localize autonomous vehicles and related parts
    Promotion of convergence technology development (convergence sensing, such as camera, radar, and LiDAR, and AI-based driving control technology) and support for mobility service demonstration (USD 900 million worth of preliminary feasibility study in progress)
    Core parts : Front and side image sensor modules, 77/79 GHz dual-band radar, low-cost LiDAR sensors, accident data recorders (ADR), V2X telecommunication technology, digital maps, entry-level complex positioning, driver/vehicle interfaces (HVI module), integrated DCU (Domain Control Unit)
  • Transition to Future Vehicles Through Special Strategies by Region Open
    Transition to Future Vehicles Through Special Strategies by Region
    The Korean automobile industry is scattered throughout the country. The industry is divided into a cluster in the west coast including the metropolitan area, a cluster in the southeast area centered on Ulsan, and a cluster in the Honam area, including Gwangju and Gunsan.
    Kia Motors and GM Korea have finished car factories located in the metropolitan area, and there are also research centers and factories, including Hyundai-Kia's Namyang Research Center, as well as a test city for autonomous vehicles (K-City). In the Chungcheong region, 124 primary suppliers are concentrated in Cheonan, Asan, and Dangjin. In the southeast region, major domestic automakers, such as Hyundai Motor Company, Renault-Samsung Motors, and GM Korea, and 337 primary parts makers form a cluster.

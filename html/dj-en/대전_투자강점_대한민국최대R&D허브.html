Promotion of a New Growth Center for Global Basic Science

  • Establishing models for basic, applied and development research, and commercialization
  • Aims to strengthen the function as an innovative industry-academia-research cluster
  • Site spans Sin-dong and Dungok-dong in Yuseong-gu, Daejeon Metropolitan City
  • By building large-scale basic research facilities and equipment, such as the heavy ion accelerator RAON, the local Innocluster will open a new paradigm for basic research.

Hub Districts

  • Serve as hubs for basic research sectors, facilities include IBS and RAON
  • Serve as a global center for scientific exchange and research based on the function as a global basic research hub

Function Districts

  • Connect and expand the research accomplishments of hub districts
  • Cooperates with hub districts to carry out joint, applied and development research, exchange human resources, and commercialization activities.

Institute for Basic Science (IBS)

  • A core research institute of the science business belt that carries out world-class research
  • Established in 2011, IBS will be gradually developed into a research institute with 50 research groups and 3,000 full-time employees by 2021
  • Conducts creative and adventurous research based on the curiosity of its researchers, and actively supports the dreams and passions of scientists who gathered from different corners of the world

Heavy Ion Accelerator RAON

  • Accelerates an ion of any element on earth heavier than hydrogen or helium to nearly light speed
  • Capable of mass-producing rare radioactive isotopes by colliding an accelerated heavy ion into the nucleus of a target atom
  • Enables the creation of new elements and research on the properties of matter by using particles to explore particles smaller than the atom

Multipurpose Research Facilities for Various Science and Technology Fields (nuclear physics, astrophysics, nuclear power, biology, medical sciences, atomic physics, and solid-state physics)

  • World’s first particle accelerator that combines a linear accelerator and a synchrotron, enabling the production of rarer isotopes.
  • Supports research on the evolution of the universe and stars, research on symmetric energy of the nucleus, the development of advanced cancer treatment technology, and research on life sciences and DNA structures
  • Revitalizes industries and markets of various fields, including cancer treatment, daily necessities, precision control, superconductivity, magnetic resonance imaging, new material development

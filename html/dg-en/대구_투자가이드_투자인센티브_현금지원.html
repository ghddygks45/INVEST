Cash Grant

For foreign investments that meet certain requirements, the government and the local government shall provide cash grant for funds required to build new factories. In doing so, various aspects shall be considered regarding the foreign investment, such as whether or not they come with high technologies, effects of investment's technology transfer, the number of jobs created, redundancy with internal investments, adequacy of location etc.

Eligibility and Usage of Cash Grant

Eligibility
To become eligible for cash grant, the foreign investment ratio shall be over 30% and meet the following requirements:
  • 10 million USD and over of foreign investment in industry support services, businesses with high technologies, or parts, material manufacturing Greenfield investment (newly built/expanded factory facilities)
  • newly built/expanded R&D facilities in fields related to industry support services or hi-tech businesses, or research facilities of non-profit corporations invested by foreigners (10 or more full-time hired research personnel)
  • For cases when the investment amount etc. do not meet the requirements, but has a profound impact on the domestic economy, the Foreign Investment Committee shall deliberate on the matter and determine whether the case is eligible for cash grant.
    • Establishing regional headquarters of multi-national companies (multi-national companies with business presence in 3 or more countries, and controlling regions of 2 or more countries)
    • Contributing to regional economic development as a regional strategic industry
    • Providing items or services not produced domestically, or which can improve domestic industry's competitiveness through introduction of advanced technologies
Grant Rate
  • Through negotiations, the cash grant ratio shall be determined at 5% and higher of the FDI, with the upper limit determined by a closed formula.
  • As for an R&D center, FDI and R&D funds from overseas that are used for stipulated purposes shall be included in the funds to be calculated. (except for funds raised domestically)
Legal Usage
Foreign-invested companies may use the cash grant only for the following purposes:
  • Funds to support employment and training
  • Land purchase·lease
  • Construction costs
  • Foundation facilities installation cost
  • Capital goods/research equipment purchasing costs
In such cases, the purchasing amount of leased land for foreign-invested companies shall be included in the cash grant limit. However, receiving cash grant shall nullify the eligibility to be provided with leased land, or support for difference in sales price through the existing location support policy (within 50% of foreign invested amount) (choose one among the said cases).

Cash Grant Application Procedure

1) Negotiation & Support Application/Evaluation
Foreigners wishing to receive a cash grant shall submit the application and investment plan to the Ministry of Knowledge Economy at which time an evaluation team consisting of related public officials and civil experts evaluate the application and the investment plan, and submit the evaluation result report to the Ministry of Knowledge Economy.
It is possible to request a negotiation with the Ministry of Knowledge Economy prior to submitting the application. Considering that the investor may identify the possibilities and amount of grant through pre-negotiation, the pre-negotiation would be more important than the formal negotiation procedure.
Standards for Evaluating a Cash Grant
  • High degree technology & technology transfer effect
  • Redundancy with domestic investment
  • Impact on regional·national economy
  • Survivability of the project
  • Number of jobs created
  • Location adequacy

2) Support Decision and Contract Conclusion
The decision on a cash grant shall be made following deliberation or consultation by the Foreign Investment Committee (for less than 1 billion won in grant). Following the above decision, the applicant, Ministry of Knowledge Economy, and head of local government(s) is able to conclude a contract for a cash grant, and carry out the grant. The contract period shall be 10 years from the last date of grant fund payment, and the contract shall include details of grant fund payment method, acquisition, rent provisions of rent land, and grant fund post-management etc. The application for a cash grant shall not take more than 60 days to conclude the contract.

3) Payment of Cash Grant
The grant funding may be paid in a lump sum on the day of the decision, or in installments up to 10 times for the duration of up to 5 years. When paying in installments, the investment expenditure plan, implementation performance or grant fund execution performance shall be evaluated to provide the payment of grant funds within the range of the funds according to the following methods (amount and term are subject to adjustments).

Pre-Evaluation Policy
The Pre-Evaluation Policy is an active method of planned investment attraction, implemented to actively attract specific foreign investments that are expected to have a major impact on the national economy. In the policy Invest KOREA (KOTRA) pre-evaluates projects and presents recommendations to the Ministry of Knowledge Economy and the Foreign Investment Committee to determine cash grant. The policy is implemented in a dual system with the existing system of evaluating the applications, and the decision to provide cash funds is made before accepting the application.
After experts' review of candidate projects for investment attraction through cash grant, Invest KOREA (KOTRA) will create negotiation plans and submit recommendations of the Foreign Investment Committee to the Minister of Knowledge Economy. In this process, the Foreign Investment Committee deliberates to determine whether or not to provide cash grant, as well as its limits. Negotiations with the investors take place within the approved limits, within 1 year after deliberation. Contract procedures and post management etc. after reaching an agreement in the negotiation are identical to existing methods. If the investment attraction is not complete within the given time, the Ministry of Knowledge Economy may extend the period up to 1 year by taking into consideration the introduction and development status of related technologies in Korea.

Cash Grant Post-Management

Appliers' Obligations
The applier shall, directly or indirectly, manage foreign invested companies and faithfully carry out the obligations of the cash grant contract, as well as the investment expenditure plan.
The applier shall enter into insurances, or take other similar measures to make it possible to recover and replace, to a satisfactory level, all assets (including those under construction) such as building, facilities & equipments etc. Contracts for acquiring assets that receive cash grants shall be concluded in a way that utilizes the cash grant fund in the most efficient way through public tenders, official appraisals, request for 2 or more estimates etc.
A prior written consent of the Ministry of Knowledge Economy shall be secured to use the cash supported assets for purposes other than the stated business, or to transfer, exchange, loan, or provide as collateral. Also, the cash grant shall not be dealt out as dividends or royalties. The concerned foreign-invested companies may not give security for any liabilities than for business purposes.
During the contract period, the applier shall provide enough information to check on the performance of the contract and submit every year to the Ministry of Knowledge Economy a statement of accounts audited externally.
Management of Cash Grant
Site of land purchased with grant from the country and local governments towards land purchasing costs and rent shall not be disposed of within 5 years after the sales contract. If the site is disposed of within 10 years after concluding the contract, proceeds from sales shall be redeemed pro rata to the support ratio of the country and local government shall be redeemed.
To be eligible for a cash grant towards training and employment subsidies, the concerned worker for the subsidy shall have been employed for 3 years or longer. Should the concerned worker be dismissed within 3 years, the subsidy shall be collected pro rata to the period (excluding interns from science & engineering fields).
With regard to grant funds for construction, purchase of facilities and equipment, and foundation facility installation, if the actual foreign investment amount is less than the foreign investment amount stated in the cash grant contract, the grant amount shall be reduced pro rata to the difference.
Cash Grant Cancellation, Withdrawal, Reduction, Redemption etc.
For cases of application by false and improper methods, non-execution of contract obligations, and bankruptcy during the contract term making business operations impossible, measures may be taken regarding the cash grant, such as cancellation, withdrawal, reduction or redemption etc. of the grant funds. Initially, requests shall be made to fulfill the contract, and opportunities shall be provided to explain the situation. Afterwards, through the deliberation and consultation by the working level committee, measures shall be determined regarding the concerned cash grant contract and grant funds.
For cases when bankruptcy during the contract period makes business operation impossible, if the applicant requests an extension of the contract in place for returning the cash grant, the extension shall be granted, barring any special reason. In returning the funds, the amount shall be whichever is larger among the followings. In such a case, the responsibilities of returning the cash grant shall be burdened, jointly or separately, by the foreign investor or the foreign-invested companies.
  • The total amount of the cash grant for cases when business operations became impossible prior to executing the investment expenditure plan
  • The sum of 1/60 (maximum 60/60) of the total amount of the cash grant for each month that falls short of the contracted period of business for cases when business operation became impossible after executing the investment expenditure plan
  • The multiplication of contracted penalties for each person that has not been hired for cases when the contracted minimum employment obligations were not fulfilled during the contract period (possible to fulfill the minimum employment obligation by extending business period)

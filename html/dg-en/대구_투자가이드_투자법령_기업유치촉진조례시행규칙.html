Daegu Metropolitan City Enforcement Regulations on Company Support
Regulation No. 02380 Enacted on April 20, 2004
Regulation No. 02405 Amended on February 28, 2005
Regulation No. 2500 Amended on December 31, 2007
Regulation No. 2552 Amended on January 30, 2009
Regulation No. 2715 Amended on July 10, 2013 (Enforcement Regulations on the Establishment of an Administrative Agency)
Regulation No. 2730 Amended on November 11, 2013 (Administrative Audit Regulations on Protection of Private Information)
Article 1 (Purpose)
The purpose of this Regulation is to stipulate issues mandated by the Daegu Metropolitan City Ordinance on Company Support (hereinafter referred to as "Ordinance") and related issues necessary for the enforcement of the Ordinance. (Regulation no.2500 Amended on December 31, 2007)
Article 2 (Companies Eligible for Industrial Site Support)
  1. ① Companies stipulated in Paragraphs 1 and 2 of Article 5 of the Ordinance are as follows:
    1. 1. Foreign-invested companies defined in Article 2.1.6 of the Foreign Investment Promotion Act.
    2. 2. Companies that handle state-of-the-art technology and are notified by the Minister of Trade, Industry & Energy under Article 5.1 of the Industrial Development Act. (Regulation no.2552 amended on January 30, 2009)(Regulation no.2715 amended on July 10, 2013)
    3. 3. Domestic companies in the industries prescribed in Article 121-2.1.1. of the Restriction of Special Taxation Act.
    4. 4. Domestic companies in the components and materials industries pursuant to Article 2.2 of Special Act on Components and Materials Company Development.
    5. 5. Domestic companies in the cultural industry pursuant to Article 2.1 of the Basic Act on Culture Industry Promotion.
    6. 6. Domestic companies in the software industry pursuant to Article 2.2 of the Software Industry Promotion Act. [Regulation no.2500 newly established on December 31, 2007]
    7. 7. Other companies deemed necessary by the Mayor [Regulation no. 2500 newly established on December 31, 2007]
  2. ② Companies seeking to have the land purchasing cost partially subsidized pursuant to Article 5.2 of the Ordinance shall sign a land contract and submit an application form in Annex 1 to the Daegu Metropolitan City Mayor (hereinafter referred to as “the Mayor").
Article 3 (Measures to Achieve the Purpose of Company Support)
Companies which purchase lands at discounted prices or have the sales cost partially subsidized pursuant to Article 5.3 of the Ordinance shall meet the following conditions:
  1. 1. Approval shall be granted in advance from local governments or relevant budget execution agencies in order to change the choice of business.
  2. 2. Construction shall begin within nine months of the land purchase, provided that it may be deferred for another three months under unavoidable circumstances.
  3. 3. The building area shall exceed the standard building area announced by the Ministry of Trade, Industry & Energy. (Regulation no.2552 amended on January 30, 2009)(Regulation no.2715 amended on July 10, 2013)
  4. 4. The land shall not be resold within five years of the purchase, provided that the amount subsidized by the central or local governments shall be reimbursed if the land is to be resold within ten years, and to that end, the Mayor shall take necessary measures such as taking the land as a collateral security.
Article 4 (Subsidy for Factory Facilities)
  1. ① The Mayor shall seek advice from the Daegu Metropolitan Company Support Committee before making a decision on whether and how much to subsidize companies if it intends to provide subsidies pursuant to Article 6 of the Ordinance.
  2. ② Companies seeking subsidies for factory facilities pursuant to Paragraph 1 shall submit the application in the form of Annex 2 within a year of the receipt of factory registration certificate or relocation of company operations or headquarters.
  3. ③ If the company receives subsidies for rental cost under Article 6.1.1 and 2 of the Ordinance, the company shall comply Article 9.3 and 4 of the support standard of national funding for regional organizations to attract companies that is notified by the Ministry of Trade, Industry & Energy. [Regulation no.2552 amended on January 30, 2009](Regulation no.2715 amended on July 10, 2013)
Article 5 (Employment and Vocational Training Subsidies)
  1. ① The support standard and process of training subsidies under Article 9.1 of the Ordinance shall comply the support standard of national funding for the attraction of regional investment companies or the support standard of national funding for regional organizations to attract companies of the Ministry of Trade, Industry & Energy.(Regulation no.2405 amended on February 28, 2005)(Regulation no.2552 amended on January 30, 2009)(Regulation no.2715 amended on July 10, 2013)
  2. ② Companies that newly hire 20 employees (10 full-time researchers in the case of corporate research institute stipulated in Article 7 of the Technology Development Promotion Act) within three years of the registration of business in the City District or relocation from the outside to the inside of the City District are eligible for a monthly employment subsidy of up to KRW 500,000 for each extra worker for up to six months pursuant to Article 9.2 of the Ordinance. (Regulation no. 2552 amended on January 30, 2009)
  3. ③ Companies that newly hire 20 employees (10 full-time researchers in the case of corporate research institute stipulated in Article 7 of the Technology Development Promotion Act) within three years of the registration of business in the City District or relocation from the outside to the inside of the City District are eligible for a monthly vocational training subsidy of up to KRW 500,000 for each extra worker for up to six months pursuant to Article 9.3 of the Ordinance. (Regulation no. 2552 newly established on January 30, 2009)
  4. ④ Individuals seeking subsidies stated in Paragraphs 1 and 2 shall submit an application in the form of Annex 3 to the Mayor. (Regulation no. 2405 amended on February 28, 2005 and Regulation no. 2552 amended on January 30, 2009)
Article 6 (Special Assistance to Large Investors)
  1. ① Foreign and domestic companies with large-size investment, eligible for receiving special subsidies with the consent from the City Council in accordance with Article 9.3 of the Ordinance, are as follows:
    1. 1. Any company that invests at least USD 30 million. (Regulation no. 2500 amended on December 31, 2007)
    2. 2. Foreign-invested companies with investment over USD 10 million that handle the state-of-the-art technology defined by the Minister of Trade, Industry & Energy under Article 5.1 of the Industrial Development Act. (Regulation no.2500 amended on December 31, 2007)(Regulation no.2552 amended on January 30, 2009)(Regulation no.2715 amended on July 10, 2013)
    3. 3. Any company that invests at least USD 10 mil. to build new factories or expand facilities pursuant to Article 121-2.1.1 of the Restriction of Special Taxation Act (Regulation no.2500 amended on December 31, 2007)
    4. 4. Any company that invests at least USD 10 mil. to build new factories or expand facilities to produce components and materials stated in a presidential decree in accordance with Article 2.1 of the Special Act on Components and Materials (Regulation no. 2500 amended on December 31, 2007)
    5. 5. Any company that makes reinvestment or builds new research facilities for R&D effort in the culture industry prescribed in Article 2.2 of the Basic Act on Culture Industry Promotion, software industry stated in Article 2.2 of the Software Industry Promotion Act, or industries stated in Article 121-2.1.1 of the Restriction of Special Taxation Act, or non-profit organization that receives subsidies in accordance with Article 2.1.4.c of the Foreign Investment Promotion Act to build new research facilities or expand existing ones in order to conduct research and development activities, and that meets the following conditions:
      1. a. At least USD 5 million of investment
      2. b. Research team composed of at least ten full-time researchers with masters or higher degrees in the relevant fields and at least three years of experience [Regulation no. 2500 entirely revised on December 31, 2007]
    6. 6. Companies with at least 300 full-time workers [Regulation no. 2500 newly established on December 31, 2007]
    7. 7. Others deemed to have made a significant contribution to the local economy and be in need of special support by the Mayor [Regulation no. 2500 newly established on December 31, 2007]
  2. ② The amount of investment pursuant to Paragraph 1 shall be the converted amount based on the Won-USD exchange rate at the point of completion of payment in the case of foreign-invested companies, and the cost incurred by construction, facility and equipment installation, and land purchase shall be converted based on the Won-USD exchange rate at the point of application in the case of domestic companies. (Regulation no. 2500 amended on December 31, 2007 and Regulation no. 2552 amended on January 30, 2009)
  3. ③ Special subsidies shall be offered within 50/100 of total investment depending on the size of job creation, influence on the local economy, and viability of the projects.
  4. ④ Companies that wish to receive special support stated in Article 9-3 of the Ordinance shall submit an application in the form of Annex 3 to the Mayor. [Regulation no. 2500 newly established on December 31, 2007] (Regulation no. 2552 amended on January 30, 2009)
Article 7 (Subsidy for Feasibility Test)
  1. ① Subsidy for feasibility tests stipulated in Article 18-2.2 shall be offered within 50 percent of the test cost with the ceiling of KRW 500 million per company.
  2. ② Investors or investor companies that wish to receive subsidies for project feasibility tests prescribed in Paragraph 1 shall submit an application in the form of Annex 6 to the Mayor within a year of the first date of the business.
Article 8 (Eligibility for Rewards)
  1. ① Candidates eligible for rewards by industrial sector and standards for provision pursuant to Article 20.2 of the Ordinance shall be presented in annexed tables 2 and 3 respectively.
  2. ② A public official that exhibits good performance on investment promotion pursuant to Paragraph 1 may be assigned to desired division or may be promoted or win additional scores for promotion in accordance with his/her contribution, in which case a public official who gets promoted may see a cutback in bonuses based on the decision by the Company Support Committee. (Regulation no. 2552 amended on December 31, 2007)
Article 9 (Application for Rewards)
  1. ① Applicants that wish to receive rewards may report the annual performance on investment promotion to the Mayor in the form of Annex 6 every year from the time when they meet the qualifications for application under Article 8 of the Ordinance. (Regulation no. 2500 amended on December 31, 2007 and Regulation no. 2552 amended on January 30, 2009)
  2. ② Rewards shall be offered for five years including the initial year that the applicants meet the qualifications under Article 8 of the Ordinance. (Regulation no. 2500 amended on December 31, 2007)
  3. ③ Deleted (Regulation no. 2552 eliminated on January 30, 2009)
Article 10 (Exception to Rewards Recipients)
Businesses and employees related to investor companies shall be excluded from beneficiaries eligible for receiving rewards pursuant to Article 20 of the Ordinance. (Regulation no. 2500 amended on December 31, 2007)

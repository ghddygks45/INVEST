Article 1 (Purpose)
The purpose of this Ordinance is to stipulate measures to effectively attract investment from foreign and domestic companies and retain promising businesses within the Daegu metropolitan area with an aim to vitalize the economy of the Daegu Metropolitan City (hereinafter referred to as the "City") and improve its industrial structure. (Ordinance No. 3556 amended on September 30, 2002)
Article 2 (Definition)
The terms used in this Ordinance shall be defined as follows:
  1. 1. "Company support" shall refer to activities including signing an investment memorandum of understanding (MOU) or agreements with corporate investors to promote investment in the Daegu Metropolitan City (hereafter “the City District”). (Ordinance No. 3556, amended on September 30, 2002)(Ordinance No. 4313, amended on December 30, 2011)
  2. 2. An "Industrial Complex" shall refer to an industrial complex as stipulated in Article 2.5 of the Industrial Sites and Development Act
  3. 3. "The Industry Support Service Industry" refers to an array of industrial activities to provide services as stated in the Korea Standard Industrial Classification, including employment support service, job search service and labor supply, call center service and telemarketing service. [Ordinance No. 3982, newly established on October 30, 2008]
  4. 4. "The number of full-time employees" refer to the number of people who provide labor for income and fall into any of the following categories, and this may include the number of dispatched workers whose employment can be proved with objective documents such as employment contracts of dispatched workers pursuant to Article 20 of the Act on Dispatched Workers' Protection.
    1. a.Average number of employees during the most recent three months as listed in the reports on the status of earned income tax withholding submitted to the pertinent tax office in accordance with Article 185.1 of the Enforcement Decree of the Income Tax Act.
    2. b.Average number of employees during the most recent three months as listed in the reports on the payment status of dues and contribution (excluding regional pension plan subscribers pursuant to Article 10 of the National Pension Fund Act) to the national pension fund in accordance with Article 3 of the National Pension Fund Act.
    3. c.Average number of employees during the most recent three months as listed in the reports on the status of insurance premiums payment pursuant to Article 62 of the National Health Insurance Act (excluding regional pension plan subscribers pursuant to Article 6 of the National Health Insurance Act) [Ordinance No. 3982, newly established on October 30, 2008]
  5. 5.“Foreign investment” shall refer to the term defined under Article 2.1.4 of the Foreign Investment Promotion Act (hereafter “the Act”). [Ordinance No. 4313, newly established on December 30, 2011]
  6. 6. “Foreign-invested company” shall refer to the company that received investment from a foreign investor defined under Article 2.1.5 of the Act. [Ordinance No. 4313, newly established on December 30, 212]
  7. 7. “Newly built” shall refer to the establishment of a new building or the transformation of the usage of the building (head office, branches, plants, logistics facilities, research centers, etc.).[Ordinance No. 4313, newly established on December 30, 2012]
  8. 8. “Expansion” shall refer to expanding the existing building to establish more business facilities. [Ordinance No.4313, Newly established on December 30, 2011]
  9. 9. “Information service industry” shall refer to the industry that provides support services in relation to document processing, portal and internet information services based on the Korean Standard Industrial Classification.[Ordinance No. 4313, newly established on December 30, 2011]
Article 3 (Responsibility for Company Support)
  1. ① The Daegu Metropolitan City Mayor (hereinafter referred to as "the Mayor") shall take the responsibility of assisting effective company support and investment promotion.
  2. ② The Mayor shall actively assist Company Support activities by small administrative districts including counties (Gu) and wards (Gun). (Ordinance No. 3982, amended on October 30, 2008)
Article 4 (Designation of Company Support Zone)
  1. ① The Mayor may designate certain areas of City District as Company Support Zone, if the designation is deemed necessary for Company Support.
  2. ② The Mayor shall notify the following items in order to designate a Company Support Zone pursuant to Paragraph 1.
    1. 1. Title, location, and area of Company Support Zone;
    2. 2. Target businesses;
    3. 3. Issues related to sales of real estates; and
    4. 4. Matters deemed necessary by the Mayor
  3. ③ The Mayor shall reflect the detailed information of a Company Support Zone on the basic management plan of the industrial complex for the year in consultations with the designated industrial complex management institution, if a certain part of the industrial complex is to be designated as Company Support Zone.
  4. ④ In the case of attracting a company located within the City District into a Company Support Zone, the Daegu Metropolitan Company Evaluation Committee to be established in accordance with Article 14 shall evaluate and deliberate on promising companies of the following categories:
    1. 1. Companies that have state-of-the-art technologies
    2. 2. Companies that make significant contribution to the local economy
Article 5 (Support for Industrial Land)
  1. ① The Mayor shall offer sites within industrial complexes developed by the City up for sale at a price lower than the original construction cost to companies deemed to be beneficial to industrial development in the City including foreign-invested companies.
  2. ② The Mayor may provide financial assistance within the range of municipal government budget to subsidize part of the purchasing cost of land within an industrial complex developed by companies stipulated in the Regulations.
  3. ③ The Mayor shall take necessary steps to achieve the purpose of company support, for instance imposing ban on sales of the lands acquired at low prices or subsidized for a certain period of time in accordance with the provisions of Paragraphs 1 and 2. [Ordinance No. 3556, entirely revised on September 30, 2002]
Article 5-2 (Acquisition of Land for Lease)
The Mayor may acquire lands under the following conditions for the purpose of lease to foreign-invested companies.
  1. 1. Land within a Foreign Investment Zone under Article 18 of the Foreign Investment Promotion Act (hereinafter referred to as the "Act") (Ordinance No. 3602, amended on October 10, 2003)
  2. 2. Land owned by the project manager of the industrial complex development[Ordinance No. 3556 newly established on September 30, 2002]
  3. 3. Land within a Company Support Zone [Ordinance No. 3602, newly established on October 10, 2003]
Article 6 (Relocation Subsidies)
  1. ① The Mayor may provide relocation subsidies within the municipal government budget to companies moving from the outside to the inside of the City District or new facilities or expanded facilities of key businesses deemed necessary for the City by the Mayor (hereinafter referred to as "Relocated Companies") on the following items. In this case, the subsidy shall not exceed KRW 1 billion per company.
    1. 1. Cost incurred by land purchase and lease
    2. 2. Construction cost or cost incurred by land acquisition and building rent
    3. 3. Cost incurred by facilities and equipment installation
    4. 4. Cost incurred by dismantlement, transportation, re-installment of existing facilities and equipment
  2. ② Companies eligible for Relocation Subsidies prescribed in Paragraph 1 shall be as follows:
    1. 1. A manufacturer with over KRW 2 billion of cost
    2. 2. A company with KRW 1 billion of cost in businesses prescribed in Article 2.1 of the Basic Act on Culture Industry Promotion or businesses described in Article 2.2 of the Basic Act on Software Industry Promotion, or corporate research institute prescribed in Article 7 of the Technology Development Promotion Act
    3. 3. An industry support service company and information service company with over 50 full-time workers (Ordinance No.4313, amended on December 30, 2011)
    4. 4. Companies that have been relocated to the Daegu Metropolitan City from the capital area or newly built and expanded companies that fall under the support standard of national funding for the attraction of regional investment companies, which was legislated by the Minister of Trade, Industry & Energy according to the regulations of the Article 11 and 19 of Special Act on Balanced National Development.[Ordinance No. 4313, newly established on December 30, 2011] (Ordinance No. 4501, amended on July 10, 2013)
  3. ③ The focus industry that is recognized by the Mayor under provision 1 shall be determined by the Mayor through the deliberation of the Company Support Committee.
  4. ④ The details of standard and process of subsidies shall be defined as regulations. [Ordinance No. 3982, amended on October 30, 2008]
Article 7 (Financial Assistance)
The Mayor may prioritize Relocated Companies in providing subsidies from company support fund managed by the City.(Ordinance No. 3556, amended on September 30, 2002)(Ordinance No. 3982, amended on October 30, 2008)
Article 8 (Administrative Assistance)
  1. ① The Mayor may handle administrative business including permission and approval for attracting companies in cooperation with the relevant institution. (Ordinance No. 3982, amended on October 30, 2008)(Ordinance No. 4313, amended on December 30, 2011)
  2. ② The details of the administrative business under provision 1 are as follows: [Ordinance No. 4313, newly established on December 30, 2011]
    1. 1. Civil affairs administration related to the establishment and expansion of a plant;
    2. 2. Lease and registration of a plant;
    3. 3. Lease of a plant to foreigners; and
    4. 4. Other difficulties and proposals addressed by corporate investors.
Article 9 (Employment Subsidy)
  1. ① The Mayor may provide subsidies for employment or vocational training within the range of municipal government budget to foreign-invested companies within the City District prescribed in Article 14.4 of the Foreign Investment Promotion Act or companies that move from the Seoul Metropolitan Area to the City District as prescribed in Article 19 of the Special Act on Balanced National Development. (Ordinance No. 3556 amended on September 30, 2002)(Ordinance No. 3602, amended on October 10, 2003)(Ordinance No. 3671, amended on October 30, 2004)(Ordinance No. 3879, amended on October 30, 2007)
  2. ② The Mayor may provide employment subsidies within municipal government budget to companies that build new operations jobs, barring those prescribed in Paragraph 1.[Ordinance No. 3671 newly established on October 30, 2004] (Ordinance No. 3879, amended on October 30, 2007)
  3. ③ The Mayor may offer subsidies for vocational training within the range of municipal government budget to companies that build new operations or relocate operations, and train newly hired full-time employees as skilled workforce, barring those prescribed in Paragraph 1.[ Ordinance No. 3671, newly established on October 30, 2004](Ordinance no. 3879 amended on October 30, 2007)
  4. ④ Businesses eligible for subsidies pursuant to Articles 2 and 3 shall be subject to the provisions of Article 63.2.1 of the Restriction of Special Taxation Act.( Ordinance No.3879, entirely revised on October 30, 2007)
  5. ⑤ Detailed information on employment and vocational training subsidies including procedures and criteria of subsidy provision shall be stated in the Regulations.(Ordinance No.3556, amended on September 30, 2002)
Article 9-2 (Support for facility development of foreign investment environment)
The Mayor shall support part of the facility cost and operation cost including the purchasing cost of land for facilities and construction cost within the budget for the following facilities: [Ordinance No. 3556, newly established on September 30, 2002][Ordinance No. 4313, amended on December 30, 2011]
  1. 1. Facilities defined under Article 2.8 of the Enforcement Decree of the Foreign Investment Promotion Act
  2. 2. Foreign education institutions under Article 22.1 of the Special Act on Designation and Management of Free Economic Zones
Article 9-3 (Special Support for Large Investors)
  1. ① The Mayor may offer special support on the newly establishment and expansion of facilities, with the consent of the City Council, to large foreign and domestic investors deemed to have significant influence on the local economy. (Ordinance No. 4313, amended on December 30, 2011)
  2. ② The scope of special support for the large foreign and domestic investors under provision 1 will be regulated according to the ripple effect of the regional economy, job creation effect, new growth strategic industry of the City and its compatibility, technical skills, the soundness of capital, business growth and the feasibility of business plans. [Ordinance No. 3602, newly established on October 10, 2003](Ordinance No. 4313, amended on December 30, 2011)
  3. ③ The scope of large-scale investment are as follows:[Ordinance No. 4313, newly established on December 30, 2011]
    1. 1. If the investment of the foreign-invested company exceeds USD 30 million or the number of full-time employees is over 300;
    2. 2. If the investment of the domestic company exceeds USD 20 million or the number of full-time employees is over 300;
    3. 3. If the corporate investor that newly establishes or expands plant facilities to conduct business defined under Article 121.2.1.1 of the Restriction of Special Taxation Act has more than USD 10 million;
    4. 4. If the company that handles advanced technology indicated by the Minister of Trade, Industry & Energy under Article 5.1 of the Industrial Development Act has more than USD 10 million; (Ordinance No. 4501, amended on July 10, 2013)
    5. 5. If the company that newly establishes or expands plants to produce components and materials decided by a Presidential decree under Article 2.1 of the Act on Special Measures for the Promotion of Specialized Enterprises, etc. for Components and Materials has more than USD 10 million;
    6. 6. If the corporate investor newly establishes or expands a new research center to conduct research and development as a cultural industry under Article 2.1 of the Framework Act on the Promotion of Cultural Industries, software industry under Article 2.2 of the Software Industry Promotion Act, business under Article 121.2.1.1 of the Restriction of Special Taxation Act and non-profit organization under Article 2.1.4 of the Foreign Investment Promotion Act;
      1. a. The investment shall be over USD 5 million;
      2. b. The research group shall be composed of more than 10 full-time employees who have more than three years of research experience with M.As in relevant industries or B.As in relevant fields; and
    7. 7. A large company that greatly affects the regional economy and is recognized by the Mayor that it needs special support.
Article 10 (Establishment of Company Support Committee)
  1. ① The Daegu Metropolitan City Company Support Committee (hereinafter referred to as the "Support Committee) shall be established to effectively support and attract companies.
  2. ② The Support Committee shall be responsible for the following:
    1. 1. Advice on the company support policies of the City including designation of Company Support Zones and target businesses
    2. 2. Activities to attract businesses, related institutions and organizations
    3. 3. Provision of rewards for inducement of foreign and domestic investment and company support (Ordinance No. 3879, entirely revised on October 30, 2007)
    4. 4. Service for the FDI promotion council in accordance with Article 23 of the Enforcement Decree of the Foreign Investment Promotion Act [Ordinance No.3602 newly established on October 10, 2003](Ordinance No. 3879, amended on October 30, 2007)
    5. 5. Advice on target business selection and support [Ordinance No. 3879, newly established on October 30, 2007]
    6. 6. Deliberation on other issues requested by the Mayor with respect to company support (Ordinance No. 3879, amended on October 30, 2007)
Article 11 (Organization of Support Committee)
  1. ① The Support Committee shall be composed of fewer than 20 commissioners including one chairman whose position shall be taken by the Mayor and one vice chairman who shall be appointed among commissioners by the chairman. (Ordinance No. 3556, amended on September 30, 2002)(Ordinance No. 3602, amended on October 10, 2003)
  2. ② The City Director of Bureau for Economic Affairs shall become an ex-officio commissioner, and the Mayor shall appoint other commissioners among those who satisfy any of the following conditions. (Ordinance No. 3982, amended on October 30, 2008)
    1. 1. City councilor recommended by the Daegu Metropolitan Council;
    2. 2. An official working for an economic institution or agency;
    3. 3. Deleted (Eliminated from Ordinance No. 3982 on October 30, 2008)
    4. 4. Officials of the City and smaller administrative districts, namely gu, and gun, or experts on issues presented by the Council; or
    5. 5. Others deemed to be helpful for company support by the Mayor [Ordinance No. 3879, newly revised on October 30, 2007]
  3. ③ The term in office as an appointed commissioner shall be two years, and it may be extended once for another two years, provided that appointed commissioners shall be released or dismissed from the position upon the closure of the last meeting of the final year in accordance with Article 2.4. (Ordinance No. 3879, amended on October 30, 2007)
  4. ④ A director shall be named to oversee affairs of the Support Committees, and the director shall serve as an administrative official of the City government for investment promotion. (Ordinance No.3602, amended on October 10, 2003)(Ordinance No. 3879, amended on October 30, 2007)
  5. ⑤ A working group shall be organized to review and coordinate issues to be deliberated by the Support Committee.[ Ordinance No. 3982, amended on October 30, 2008]
Article 12 (Company Support Committee Chairman's Responsibility)
  1. ① The Chairman of the Company Support Committee represents the Support Committee and governs general affairs the Committee.
  2. ② The Vice Chairman assists the Chairman and takes over the responsibility of the Chairman if the Chairman is unable to carry on his or her duty under unavoidable circumstances.
Article 13 (Meeting)
  1. ① Meetings of the Support Committee shall be convened when deemed necessary by the Chairman, and the Chairman shall be the chairperson of the meeting.
  2. ② A quorum for a meeting of the Committee shall be the presence of a majority of all members then in office and all resolutions shall be adopted by the affirmative vote of the majority of the members present.
  3. ③ The Mayor shall have non-commissioners attend the meeting, speak before the Committee, or submit a report, if necessary.
Article 14 (Establishment of Company Support Evaluation Committee)
  1. ① The Daegu Metropolitan Company Support Evaluation Committee (hereinafter referred to as the "Evaluation Committee") shall be organized for fair evaluation and selection of companies.
  2. ② The Evaluation Committee evaluates companies and deliberates on the following items:
    1. 1. Feasibility of business plans
    2. 2. Technological excellence
    3. 3. Management capability
    4. 4. Contribution to the local economy
    5. 5. Other issues deemed necessary for the selection of companies
  3. ③ In accordance with Article 10.2.5 of the Ordinance, the City may forgo evaluation or deliberation on the companies that have already been examined by the Evaluation Committee pursuant to Article 14.2 of the Ordinance. [Ordinance No.3879, newly Established on October 30, 2007]
Article 15 (Organization of Evaluation Committee)
  1. ① The Evaluation Committee shall be composed of 15 or fewer members including one chairman and one vice chairman, and both of them shall be named among commissioners.
  2. ② The Mayor shall appoint the commissioners who satisfy any of the following conditions.
    1. 1. One city councilor recommended by the Deagu Metropolitan City Council
    2. 2. Six technical experts
    3. 3. Four management experts
    4. 4. Three city government officials
    5. 5. One person working for an economic institution
  3. ③ Members shall be named or appointed every time a meeting is convened to discuss items on the agenda, and be released or dismissed from the position upon the closure of the meeting.
  4. ④ Commissioners of the Evaluation Committee shall not double as commissioner of the Support Committee, barring exceptions recognized by the Mayor.(Ordinance No.3879,amended on October 30, 2007)(Ordinance No. 3982, amended on October 30, 2008)
  5. ⑤ A director shall be appointed to oversee affairs of the Evaluation Committee and the head of the City Industrial Complex Team shall take the responsibility. (Ordinance No. 3602, amended on October 10, 2003)(Ordinance No.3830, amended on January 8, 2007)(Ordinance No. 3879, amended on October 30, 2007) (Ordinance No. 3959, amended on August 5, 2008)
Article 16 (Responsibility of Evaluation Committee Chairman)
  1. ① The Chairman of the Evaluation Committee shall represent the Evaluation Committee and govern general affairs of the Committee.
  2. ② Vice Chairman shall assist the Chairman and take over the responsibility of the chairman if the chairman is unable to carry on his or her duty owing to unavoidable circumstances.
Article 17 (Meeting)
  1. ① Meetings of the Evaluation Committee shall be convened if deemed necessary by the chairman, and the chairman shall be a chairperson of the meeting.
  2. ② A quorum for a meeting of the Evaluation Committee shall be the presence of a majority of all members then in office and all resolutions shall be adopted by the affirmative vote of the majority of the members present.
  3. ③ The chairman shall report the result of the meeting to the Mayor without delay and the Mayor shall take necessary actions so that the result shall be reflected in selection of companies.
  4. ④ The Mayor shall have non-commissioners attend the meeting, speak before the committee, or submit a report if necessary.
Article 18 (Benefits)
  1. ① Commissioners of the Support Committee and Evaluation Committee may be offered benefits and allowances in accordance with the Daegu Metropolitan City Ordinance on Organization and Management of Committees. (Ordinance No. 3879, amended on October 30, 2007)
  2. ② Non-commissioners who attend meetings of the Support Committee or the Evaluation Committee may receive attendance allowances within the range of municipal government budget.
Article 18-2 (Support for Expenses)
  1. ① The Mayor may seek advice with respect to large investment projects, SOC, introduction of technologies, or investment promotion from investment experts or companies specialized in company support such as consulting service providers. In that case, allowances may be offered to subsidize consulting fees and expenses based on the business performance according to a business agreement.
  2. ② The Mayor may offer financial assistance within the range of municipal government budget to foreign and domestic investor companies which conduct feasibility test for new investment or reinvestment, and launch businesses. [Ordinance No. 3602, newly established on October 10, 2003]
Article 18-3 (Foreign Investment Promotion Advisor)
  1. ① The foreign investment promotion officer defiend under Article 16 of the Foreign Investment Promotion Act shall be the Director-General of Economy and Trade and the work of Foreign Investment Promotion Office under Article 22 of the Enforcement Ordinance of the Foreign Investment Promotion Act shall be conducted by the Investment Promotion Group.(Ordinance No. 3830, amended on January 8, 2007)(Ordinance No. 3879, amended on October 30, 2007)(Ordinance No. 3959, amended on August 5, 2008)(Ordinance No. 4313, amended on December 30, 2011)
  2. ② The Mayor may request labor supply from related institutions, or counties (Gu) and wards (Gun) for effective investment promotion activities by the Foreign Investment Promotion Advisor in accordance with Paragraph 1. [Ordinance No. 3602, newly established on October 10, 2003]
Article 18-4 (Dispatch of Private Expert)
  1. ① The Mayor may ask private companies or investment promotion agencies to dispatch experts to attract investment more effectively.
  2. ② Dispatched workers may receive expenses for investment promotion activities within the range of municipal government budget and may use City-owned resources including accommodations in accordance with Paragraph 1. [Ordinance No. 3671, newly established on October 30, 2004]
Article 18-5 (Appointment of Company Support Advisor)
  1. ① The Mayor may appoint foreign and domestic investment promotion experts as company support advisors in order to fully tap on excellent human resources from the private sector.
  2. ② Company support advisors prescribed in Paragraph 1 may receive allowances or benefits within the range of municipal government budget.[Ordinance No. 3671, newly established on October 30, 2004]
  3. ③ Company support advisors shall engage in investment promotion activities, collect or provide data, or answer inquiries by the Mayor. [Ordinance No. 3879, newly established on October 30, 2007]
Article 19 (Aftercare)
  1. ① The Mayor shall protect and support companies by identifying and resolving their grievances.
  2. ② The Mayor shall request companies and the interested parties that received support to submit relevant documents including yearly investment plans, results and aftercare management. When necessary, the relevant civil servant shall carry out on-site confirmation of the documents. [Ordinance No. 3602, newly established October 10, 2003](Ordinance No. 4313, December 30, 2011)
Article 19-2 (Cancellation and Refund of Subsidies)
  1. ① The Mayor shall demand full or partial refund of subsidies from beneficiaries if the results of the recipient fail to meet the yearly investment plans and the recipient shall fully or partially refund the subsidies under the following circumstances:(Ordinance No. 4313, December 30, 2011)
    1. 1. Receiving subsidies via a false means and methods;
    2. 2. Relocating factories to other cities, suspending, or closing businesses without a just reason within five years of the first business day. (Ordinance No. 3879, amended on October 30, 2007);
    3. 3. Giving up or canceling businesses for which the beneficiary received subsidies;
    4. 4. Idling factories within two years after the completion of construction without a just reason; or
    5. 5. Any other cases considered unable to achieve the goal of subsidies
  2. ② Companies that received employment or vocational training subsidies shall maintain the number of employees eligible for subsidies for at least three years, and if the number was reduced within three years, the Mayor may have the subsidies fully or partially refunded corresponding to the labor reduction.
  3. ③ Subsidies to be refunded pursuant to Paragraphs 1 and 2 shall be paid back in accordance with local tax regulations. [Ordinance No. 3671, newly established on October 30, 2004]
Article 20 (Rewards for Investment Promotion) (Ordinance No. 3982 amended on October 30, 2008)
  1. ① The Mayor may offer rewards within the range of municipal government budget to anyone deemed to have made a significant contribution to promoting foreign and domestic investment or attracting companies to the City. (Ordinance No. 3879, amended on October 30, 2007) (Ordinance No. 3982, amended on October 30, 2008)
  2. ② Rewards pursuant to Paragraph 1 shall be given in accordance with annual performance on investment promotion, and shall have a ceiling of KRW 100 million for individuals, corporations, organizations, and public officials. Individuals, corporations, and organizations, who have signed agreements on investment promotion with the City, may receive the amount within 1 percent of the confirmed investment. Detailed information and criteria including business types and share of rewards shall be stipulated in the Regulations. [Ordinance No.3602, entirely revised on October 10, 2003]. (Ordinance No. 3879, amended on October 30, 2007)
Article 21 Application of other ordinances)
Any other issues with respect to subsidies shall be stipulated in the Daegu Metropolitan City Subsidy Management Ordinance.
Article 21-2 (Confidentiality and Non-disclosure of Meetings)
  1. ① Anyone who attend meetings for corporate promotion shall not disclose any document or information on the memorandum of understanding (MOU), agreements, contents of consulting, investment promotion information and potential investors.
  2. ② The meetings on corporate promotion shall be carried out behind closed doors in accordance with provision 1. [Ordinance No. 4313, newly established on December 30, 2011]
Article 22 (Enforcement Regulations)
Any issues deemed necessary for the execution of this Ordinance shall be stipulated in the Enforcement Regulations.

Jeolla Bukdo(North Jeolla Province) Application Criteria for Investment Subsidies

Common criteria
  • ① Companies that sign a preliminary MOU with Jeollabuk-do (prior to investment)
  • Companies that have maintained their business for 3 years or longer (large-scale investments are excluded)
    * Separate provisions that apply to businesses in their operation less than 3 years were newly inserted.
  • ③ Investments of over KRW 1 billion (over KRW 100 million for IT, CT, R&D companies, carbon enterprises, and reshoring companies)

Criteria for subsidies to facilitate: Guidelines for subsidy criteria
Requirement [Common requirements: Shall sign a contract with local government prior to the actual date of commencing investment
/ Shall incorporate separate entities involved in (manufacturing, ICT, knowledge service) ]
▶A company moving from capital areas(Article 9):
① Shall sustain its operation with 30 or more full-time employees over three years in a designated site within or adjacent to capital areas, before moving into the provincial districts.
② Shall fall under all the following categories:
  • Where the company moves the rest of its business located within or near capital areas into the provincial districts,
  • Where the company ensures its business consistency,
  • Where the company hires 30 or more employees,
  • Where the company completes the process of selling or closing its business before moving into the provincial districts

※ Judicial exceptions
A company hiring 10 or more full-time employees shall fall under all the following categories:
  • Where the company involves in key industries, knowledge service, flagship industries of the National Innovation-Convergence cluster, high-technology, business where local authorities encourage investment into, business associated with the state-led metropolitan economic partnership program,
  • Where the company resettles into Growth Promotion Area, Special Industrial Crisis Response Zone or within city-provincial districts nominated as Special Industrial Crisis Response Zone
▶ A company newly establishing or building out production plants (Article 10):
① Shall sustain its operation with 10 or more full-time employees over three years in Korea, before moving into the provincial districts.
(* the company shall have a pre-existing office in operation for a year or more within the site to build out its facility)
② Shall fall under all the following categories:
  • Where the company involves in business associated with the state-led metropolitan economic partnership program, key industries, business where local authorities encourage investment into, high-technology, flagship industries of national innovation-convergence cluster,
  • Where the company hits employment rate over 10% (at least 10 full-time workers),
  • Where the company invests KRW1 billion or more,
  • Where the company keeps running its pre-existing business within the districts (No sale, shutdown, lease, downscale)
▶ A company returning its business back to Korea (Article 11):
① Shall fall under all the following categories:
  • Where the company is part of nominees to benefit from government initiatives to support reshoring before commencing investment,
  • Where the company is regarded as a mid or small sized enterprise,
  • Where the company hires 30 or more full-time employees

② Shall fall under all the following categories:
  • Where the company hires 30 or more full-time employees after investing into the provincial districts;
  • Where the company keeps running business, exactly the same as what it used to do overseas;
  • Where the company hands over or shuts down its business overseas within 4 years from the moment of reshoring
    ※Judicial exceptions)
A company hiring 10 or more full-time employees shall fall under all the following categories:

Support policies for reshoring companies coming to North Jeolla Province

The current status of companies bringing business back to North Jeolla Province
  • Number of reshoring companies authorized to move into the provincial districts: 16 (out of 69 throughout the country)
    • Reshoring places: North Jeolla Province(16), Busan city(12), Gyeonggi / North Gyeongsang Province(8), South Chungcheong Province(7), Sejong city(5), Others(2)
    • Business categories: Jewelry(77%), Textile(11%), Others(12%)
    • Current status of reshoring companies in North Jeolla Province (16 enterprises): Jewlry(11), Textile(2), Electronics (2), Chemical products(1)
  • Formed Iksan Fashion Jewelry Joint Research and Development Center (Jul 2018: an officially authorized certificate body which helps companies residing in the center improve product credibility and competitiveness) which has acquired the title of a certificate authority issuing a reliable license worldwide for two years(Mar 2020)
Provincial government’s support policies
  • Cover 10% of investments for companies financing over KRW100 million and hiring 5 or more full-time employees
    ※ Provide each enterprise with incentives worth up to KRW30 billion
  • Provide wage subsidies: KRW7.2 million per person annually (for 2 years), covering up to 100 new staffs
  • Allow tax deduction: 50~100% of income / corporate tax for up to 7 years, 50~100% customs duties
  • Allow private purchase contract legally acceptable when acquiring public properties and lease (for up to 50 years), Allow deductions on tax and rent paid for lease (up to 100%)
  • Give priority to be incentivized to a business which renovates abandoned factories inside the national industry clusters and facilitates production plants within the provincial districts
<Summary of the currently available incentives for reshoring companies>
  • Tax break (densely populated capital areas excluded)
    • (corporate tax/ income tax) lower 50~100% of tax liability for up to 7 years targeting candidates newly establishing or building out production plants in Korea)
    • (customs duties) subtract up to 100% of taxable income for an investor accommodate facilities
  • Subsidies to facilitate investment into local districts (non-capital area): 9~40% of land values, 6~24% of facility costs (within the budget limit up to KRW10 billion)
  • Wage subsidies(Mid-sized & small enterprises): KRW7.2 million per person annually (for two years), covering up to 100 new staffs
  • Manpower support services: employment permit system(EPS), E9 VISA
  • Smart factory: increase threshold for financial incentives for reshoring companies (for candidates newly establishing facilities: KRW100mln → KRW150mln, for investors further advancing supply system and manufacturing practices: KRW150mln → KRW200mln)
  • Loan services (corporate financing services): support policies worth KRW4.5 trillion for candidates investing in facilities, corporate guarantee for reshoring companies
  • R&D: Give priority to reshoring companies for R&D incentives
